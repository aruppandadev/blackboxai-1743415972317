Algebra Formulas

Quadratic Equations

The quadratic formula is essential for solving equations of the form ax² + bx + c = 0:

x = [-b ± √(b²-4ac)]/2a

Remember that the discriminant (b²-4ac) tells you about the nature of the roots:

  • Positive discriminant: Two real roots
  • Zero discriminant: One real root
  • Negative discriminant: Two complex roots

Exponent Rules

Product Rule xᵃ · xᵇ = xᵃ⁺ᵇ
Quotient Rule xᵃ / xᵇ = xᵃ⁻ᵇ
Power Rule (xᵃ)ᵇ = xᵃᵇ

Linear Functions

The slope-intercept form is:

y = mx + b

Where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept. For two points (x₁,y₁) and (x₂,y₂), the slope is:

m = (y₂ - y₁)/(x₂ - x₁)
